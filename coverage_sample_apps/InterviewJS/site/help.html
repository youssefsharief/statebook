How to use the chat dashboard:

Start by transcribing your interview notes into th left interview panel.

If you already have a transcript you can copy and paste it into the panel.


1. Create a simple question and answer exchange

  • Look to the left and highlight a quote in the transcript so it appears bottom left of the interviewee panel. Leave it here for now.
  • Add a question
    • Look to the right and select ‘script a question’.
    • Type a question here that would lead into the quote you have highlighted or chose a pre-scripted option.
    • Press enter so it appears in the bottom right panel.
    • Click the green + to add the question to the central chat panel
  • Add a response
    • Go back to the interviewee panel on the left.
    • Click the green + to add the quote as a response to the question in the central chat.
  • Great, you’ve just started your story!

    • NOTE: Be aware of fake news and copyright protection. You can add a link to the source of your quote via the box in the interviewee panel.

2. Ask two questions and let the user choose

  • Identify two quotes in your transcript.
  • Add questions
    • Write your first question and hit ‘enter’ to add it to the bottom panel.
    • Select ‘second choice’ to add a second question.
    • Use the green + to add both questions to the central chat panel
  • Add responses
    • Highlight the answer for the question on the right first and use the green + to add it to the central chat panel.
    • Then highlight the answer for the question on the left second and use the green + to add it to the central chat panel.
    • The reader will only receive the answer to the question that s/he selects.

    NOTE: There is no point asking a question if you don’t have an answer. Keep working left to right, select answers first before building a question.

3. Create multimedia questions and responses

  • Video, maps, images, sounds, and other multimedia make your conversations interactive - so use as many as you can!
  • Add multimedia
    • Explore the tabs on top of the interviewee panel and decide on the response e.g. a video or map.
    • Add a link or iframe and watch it appear in the bottom left panel
    • Use the green + to add it to the central chat panel
  • Add a question
    • Create an interaction that leads into the multimedia response.
    • For multimedia questions use the tabs at the top of the end-user panel. Experiment with using display text – for a video link it could read “Any thoughts about this clip?”
    • Use the green + to add it to the central chat panel

    NOTE: Try to use iframes rather than links - they create a preview image in the central chat panel. You can find iframes via the share icon [embed].

4. Edit the text of chat messages

  • Place your cursor over the message that you want to edit.
  • Select the three dots to the left.
  • Click delete or edit and the bubble will begin to wriggle.
  • Edit the text in the bottom left corner of the interviewee panel or add a link with a source.

    • You can edit your interviewees’ speech for clarity but remember: journalism rules apply!

5. Rearrange the order of your conversation

  • Place your cursor on a chat message, drag and drop it to another position.

6. Think about the bigger picture

  • You can jump between interviewees by clicking the round icons on top of the central chat panel.
  • If you want to change the story set-up or context, simple select ‘story elements’ on top of your page.
  • Once you’ve completed all your interviews select “review story” to proceed to publish.
  • You will be able to edit your story even after it’s been published.
